Historical Timeline

  • 18th Century: The Age of Enlightenment and the Dawn of the Industrial Revolution

    1700s

    Background: Enlightenment ideals emphasized reason, science, and individual freedoms. The Industrial Revolution began in the late 18th century, transitioning from manual to machine-based production. Britain established itself as a maritime power with colonial expansion.

    Impact on London: London saw economic prosperity, population growth, cultural flourishing, and social inequality during this period.

  • 19th Century: The Victorian Era

    1837–1901

    Background: The Industrial Revolution peaked, establishing Britain as the "workshop of the world." Queen Victoria's reign saw Britain’s global influence expand alongside advances in science and technology.

    Impact on London: The city underwent industrial and infrastructure development, faced social challenges, and became a cultural hub while experiencing urban renewal.

  • Early 20th Century: The Edwardian Era and the Pre-War Period

    1901–1910

    Background: The Edwardian period blended opulence with social change. Labor movements and women’s suffrage campaigns reflected societal tensions.

    Impact on London: London was a cultural and fashion capital, a focal point for social movements, and a city bracing for global conflict.
